Active Disturbance Rejection Force Control for Quadruped Robots under Interaction Uncertainties

Bingyun Liang*, Jinya Su*,
Institution Name
Conference name and year
Paper Code

We demonstrate active disturbance rejection force control for quadruped robots (Unitree Go2) under payload, external pushes, and interaction uncertainties.

Abstract

Physical interaction tasks expose quadruped robots to payload variations, external pulls/pushes, and actuation losses, which appear as unknown disturbances in the robot dynamics and can substantially degrade force-control performance. This paper presents a generalized extended state observer (GESO)-based force control framework for quadruped locomotion under unknown interaction uncertainties. A computationally efficient GESO is designed to estimate the lumped disturbances in real time, and the estimates are injected into an existing QP-based force controller as feedforward compensation. In this way, the proposed framework preserves the simplicity and high control frequency of conventional model-based force control while endowing it with active disturbance rejection capability, without redesigning the nominal controller. The method is experimentally validated on a Unitree Go2 robot in standing, pulling, straight-trotting, and circular-trotting tasks, and is further stress-tested in high-fidelity simulation under severe asymmetric payload and high-inertia-leg scenarios. Hardware results show that, compared with the baseline controller, the proposed method reduces the height RMSE by 88.77% in the payload-standing task and by 85.74% in circular trotting, decreases the final x-axis offset by 95.90% in the pulling task, and lowers the roll RMSE by 78.93% during circular trotting, while also improving overall trajectory tracking. Both hardware and simulation results demonstrate significantly enhanced posture regulation, tracking performance, and robustness under unknown interaction uncertainties.
